Park Güell

Park Güell was to be a residential park intended for sixty single family residences. The project failed and the park became city property in 1923. Gaudí directed the constuction of the park from 1900-1914 for Eusebi Güell. One house not built by Gaudí was built as a show house. As there was no interest Eusebi Güell convinced Gaudi to buy the house so he moved in with his family.The centerpiece is a forest of fluted columns intended as a market for the residents. It is covered by an ergonomic snaking bench encrusted with ceramic shards of all colors and patterns. Meant to be a place for social gatherings and entertainment it offers tourists a place to relax and enjoy a view of the city.










Casa Batlló

Casa Batlló was redesigned in 1904 by Gaudí for Josep Batlló a wealthy aristocrat. Gaudí was inspired by colors found in corals and marine life for the facade of the building with balconies that resemble skulls. Josep Batlló lived with his family in the lower floors and rented the others. Today Casa Batlló is open for tours and private events.





Casa Milà

Casa Milà is also known as La Pedrera due to it's undulating stone facade resembling a quarry. Built between 1906-1912 La Pedrera is regarded innovative due to its functional, constructive and ornamental aspects and therefore considered a breakthrough work of 20th century architecture. It was Gaudí's last civil work. It is currently the headquarters of the Catalunya-La Pedrera Foundation which manages public tours, exhibitions and cultural events.
